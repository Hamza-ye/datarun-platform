# 05: Periodic Visits and In-Person Assessment

Someone is expected to visit a place or person on a regular basis to observe, verify, and assess how things are going. These visits are planned ahead of time but may happen late, get rescheduled, or be missed entirely.

During the visit, the visitor looks at specific aspects of the work, notes what they find, and may identify things that need to be corrected or followed up on. What they find during one visit may influence what they look for next time.

This results in:

* A record of each visit — when it happened, what was observed, what was noted
* Visibility into whether planned visits actually took place
* A history of observations that shows how things change over time
* Awareness of issues that were found and whether they were addressed
