# 08: Following Something Over Time Until It's Resolved

Some situations require ongoing attention. A problem is identified, and it doesn't get resolved in a single step — it requires multiple interactions, decisions, and follow-ups over days, weeks, or longer.

Different people may be involved at different points. Responsibility for what happens next may shift from one person to another. Each interaction — a visit, a decision, a note — adds to the record of that situation. Eventually, the situation reaches a conclusion, but until then it remains active and visible.

This results in:

* An ongoing record of a situation from the moment it was identified until it was resolved
* A trail of who did what, when, and what happened next
* Visibility into what's still active, what's been resolved, and what's been waiting too long
