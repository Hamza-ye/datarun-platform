# 18: When Patterns in Past Work Trigger New Work

Over time, the accumulated history of observations and activities may reveal patterns that humans wouldn't easily spot — anomalies, correlations, emerging trends, or signs of problems.

When such a pattern is detected, it may warrant a new effort: an investigation, a targeted campaign, a corrective action. This new effort should carry context about why it was initiated — what pattern was seen, and in which prior work — so the people involved understand why they're being asked to act.

When the new effort concludes, what was learned feeds back into future pattern detection, potentially refining or suppressing future responses.

This results in:

* New work that arises from analysis of past work, not from human planning
* Clear traceability from the triggering pattern to the resulting effort
* A feedback loop where completed work informs future detection
