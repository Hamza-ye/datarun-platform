# 12: When Something Happens, Something Else Should Follow

Certain observations or occurrences should automatically lead to a response. A stockout is reported — someone should investigate. A critical value is recorded — the right people should know. A deadline passes without action — attention should be drawn to it.

The response may involve different people than those who recorded the original information. Some situations escalate: if the initial response doesn't happen, a broader or more urgent reaction follows.

This results in:

* Responses that follow from what has been observed or recorded
* The right people becoming aware when something significant happens
* Escalation when expected responses don't occur in time
