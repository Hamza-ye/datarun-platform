# 11: Work That Must Pass Through Multiple Levels of Judgment

Some work requires agreement from more than one person before it can proceed or be considered final. Each person in the chain has their own authority and perspective. One might check accuracy, another might check compliance, a third might give strategic approval.

Each level can accept, return, or reject what they received. If any level delays, the whole chain stalls, and that delay must be visible. The full path — who saw it, what they decided, and when — must be traceable after the fact.

This results in:

* Work that progresses through a sequence of reviews, each by a different person or role
* Visibility into where in the sequence work currently sits
* A complete, auditable history of every decision made along the way
