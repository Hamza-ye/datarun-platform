# 00: Recording Structured Information

Someone needs to record a set of known details about something they've observed, done, or received.

The type and shape of information to record is known ahead of time. The person fills in the details, and the record is kept for future reference.

This results in:

* A completed record of what was observed or reported
* Confidence that the required details were captured correctly
* The ability to look up what was recorded later

---

## What makes this hard

The expected set of details may change after records have already been captured under the previous version. Some records follow the old shape, new records follow the updated one — both must remain valid and retrievable.

Different people may independently record information about the same thing, producing duplicates that are not identical. The same event might be captured twice with slightly different details, or at different times, by different people who didn't know the other had already recorded it.

A record may need to be corrected after it was considered complete. The correction must be traceable — who changed what, when, and why — without erasing the original. Corrections made while offline add further complexity: the original and the correction may arrive centrally in the wrong order, or a correction may be made to a record that has already been corrected by someone else.
