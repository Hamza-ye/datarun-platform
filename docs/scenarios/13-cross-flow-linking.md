# 13: When Separate Activities Are Related

Sometimes, information recorded in one context is relevant to another. A supply delivery relates to a distribution campaign. A patient visit connects to an earlier case investigation. A facility assessment references findings from a previous audit.

These connections matter — understanding one activity may require seeing what happened in another. Yet these activities are separate: they happen at different times, involve different people, and serve different purposes. The connection between them should be visible without forcing them into the same process.

This results in:

* The ability to see relationships between activities that are otherwise independent
* Context from one area available when working in another
* Connections that inform without creating rigid coupling between separate activities
