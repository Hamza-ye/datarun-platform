# 01: Recording Information About a Specific Thing

Someone records details about a recognizable, real-world thing — a place, a piece of equipment, an organizational unit, a person, or any other identifiable subject.

The record is tied to that specific thing, so over time it becomes possible to see what has been recorded about it, when, and by whom.

This results in:

* A record linked to a known, identifiable subject
* A growing history of observations or reports about that subject
* The ability to review everything associated with a particular thing

---

## What makes this hard

The same real-world thing may be recorded under different identities by different people — the same facility with two different names, the same person registered in two areas, the same equipment listed twice after a transfer. These duplicates accumulate over time and are rarely obvious at the moment of recording.

A thing's identity may become ambiguous. A facility splits into two, a household merges with another, a piece of equipment is reassigned and re-labeled. Records created before the change still point at the original identity; records created after point at the new one. Both sets of records need to remain meaningful.

Records about a subject that are created offline by multiple people may arrive out of order. The apparent history of a thing — what was observed about it and when — depends on sync timing, not on when the observations actually happened.
