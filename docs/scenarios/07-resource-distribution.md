# 07: Handing Things Off and Confirming Receipt

Items, supplies, or resources move from one party to another. The sender sends, the receiver confirms they got it — or reports that something is missing or wrong.

This may happen across levels: someone at a central office sends to a regional office, who sends to a local site. Each handoff involves a transfer, an acknowledgment, and potentially a discrepancy report.

This results in:

* A record of what was sent, by whom, and to whom
* Confirmation (or dispute) of what was received
* Traceability of where things went and whether they arrived as expected
