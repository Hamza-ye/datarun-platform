# 14: Moving Things Through a Chain of Responsibility

Resources, supplies, or materials originate from one place and move through multiple levels before reaching their final destination. At each level, someone receives them and passes them along to the next.

Each handoff is its own moment: a transfer, a confirmation, and potentially a discrepancy. Not everything moves at the same speed — some handoffs are delayed, some are partial, some require follow-up. The full journey of a resource, from origin to final destination, matters.

At any point in time, it should be possible to understand:

* Where things currently are
* What has already been handed off and confirmed
* What is still in transit or outstanding

The movement may be part of a broader effort (a campaign, a seasonal distribution), but the tracking of what went where and when remains consistent regardless of the purpose.

This results in:

* Traceability from origin to destination across multiple handoff points
* Visibility into the current state of movement at any point in time
* A record of each handoff, confirmation, and discrepancy along the way
