# 09: A Planned, Time-Bound Effort Across Many Places

An organization decides to carry out a coordinated effort across many locations or groups within a defined time window. The effort is planned in advance: who needs to do what, where, and by when.

The work unfolds in stages — preparation, execution, and follow-up. Different teams handle different areas. Progress is monitored throughout: what's been done, what's behind schedule, what hasn't started. People overseeing the effort can see where things stand and step in when needed.

This results in:

* A structured plan for work across many locations within a time window
* Visibility into progress, delays, and completion across all areas
* The ability for coordinators to see the whole picture and act on gaps
