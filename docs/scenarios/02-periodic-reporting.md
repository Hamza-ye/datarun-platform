# 02: Regular, Recurring Reporting

Certain information must be reported on a recurring basis — weekly, monthly, quarterly, or on some other regular rhythm.

Each responsible party is expected to report once per period. When a period passes without a report, that gap is noticeable. Over time, the pattern of who reported, when, and what was missed becomes visible.

This results in:

* A rhythm of expected reports tied to time periods
* Visibility into which reports arrived and which didn't
* A longitudinal view of reporting over time
